Training Philosophy

üî• Sam Sulek's Intensity Rules

  • True failure: "If you can rack it clean, you didn't go hard enough." Push past when your brain says stop ‚Äî that's where growth happens.
  • Stretch under load: Control the eccentric. The negative is where muscle breaks down. Own every inch on the way down.
  • Partials at the end: When you can't do full reps, do half reps. When you can't do halves, do quarter pumps. Don't just stop.
  • Drop sets: Strip the weight and keep going. Ego doesn't build muscle ‚Äî stimulus does.
  • Feel over numbers: "I don't care what the weight says. I care if the muscle is working." If you don't feel it, change the angle.
  • Machines are tools: Use machines when you need to push to absolute failure safely. No spotter? Use the Smith. No shame.

üèÜ Jay Cutler's Precision Rules

  • Own the weight: "Don't let the weight control you. YOU control the weight." If you're swinging, it's too heavy. Period.
  • Elbows lead everything: Back work? Think elbows. Pull with your elbows, not your hands. Your hands are just hooks.
  • Full ROM always: Partial reps have their place, but full range builds complete muscle. Stretch to squeeze, every rep.
  • Pose between sets: Flex the muscle you just worked. Feel where the blood went. This builds mind-muscle connection that takes years off your development.
  • Consistency beats intensity: "One crazy workout means nothing. 1000 consistent workouts builds champions."
  • Recovery IS training: Sleep 7-8 hours. Eat every 2.5-3 hours. Muscles grow outside the gym, not in it.

Exercise Execution

Incline Press

  • Sam: "30¬∞ angle. Touch high. Don't lock out ‚Äî keep the chest under tension the whole time. When you fail, do partials in the bottom half where the stretch is."
  • Jay: "Control the descent for 2-3 seconds. Press up explosively but stop short of lockout. I never clanked the weight at the top ‚Äî always controlled."
  • Why it matters: Upper chest shelf is EVERYTHING in front double bi and side chest. 45¬∞ is shoulder pressing ‚Äî lower the bench.

Lateral Raise

  • Sam: "I use 15-20 lb max. Ego doesn't build shoulders. Lead with the elbow, slight lean forward, thumbs down. Pause at the top. If you're swinging, you're cheating yourself."
  • Jay: "Seated laterals eliminate cheating. Strict form, light weight, feel every fiber. I'd do 4 sets of 15-20, dropset the last one, then flex between sets."
  • Why it matters: Side delts make or break the V-taper illusion. They're small muscles ‚Äî they don't need heavy weight, they need TENSION.

Back ‚Äî Rows

  • Sam: "Full stretch at the bottom is everything. Let the weight pull your scapula apart, then drive the elbow back. I'm not rowing to my body ‚Äî I'm driving my elbow through the wall behind me."
  • Jay: "Elbows, elbows, elbows. Your hands are hooks. Initiate with the elbow, squeeze the shoulder blade, hold for a second. That's how you build thickness."
  • Why it matters: Back double bi requires THICKNESS through the mid-back. Half reps build nothing. Full stretch, full squeeze.

Back ‚Äî Pulldowns

  • Sam: "I pull to my hip pocket for lower lats. Single-arm, stand offset. Arc the handle DOWN and IN toward your hip. This builds the sweep that makes your waist look small."
  • Jay: "Lean back 15-20¬∞, drive elbows to your hips not behind you. Don't just pull the bar down ‚Äî think 'elbows down and back.' Full stretch at top, squeeze at bottom."
  • Why it matters: Lower lat sweep creates the V-taper that separates Classic from Men's Physique. If your lats end at your waist, you'll never win.

Rear Delts

  • Sam: "Lead with pinkies, not thumbs. Slight elbow bend ‚Äî maintain it throughout. If you're swinging, the weight is too heavy. I'd rather do 15 lb for 20 perfect reps than 30 lb for 10 trash reps."
  • Jay: "Face pulls, reverse pec deck, bent-over flies ‚Äî I did rear delts 3-4 times a week. They're a weak point for everyone. High frequency, moderate weight, perfect form."
  • Why it matters: Rear double bi is 40% rear delts. Flat rear delts = flat pose. This is where shows are won or lost.

Legs ‚Äî Hamstrings First

  • Sam: "I do hams before quads. They're usually weaker and people skip them when fatigued. Point toes down on leg curls ‚Äî more hamstring, less calf."
  • Jay: "Lying leg curls, seated curls, I'd do both. Full stretch at the top, squeeze at the bottom. Rest-pause on the last set to get extra volume without extra fatigue."
  • Why it matters: Classic requires complete leg development. Judges see you from the side ‚Äî flat hamstrings ruin the taper from glutes to knee.

Calves

  • Sam: "Deep stretch at the bottom ‚Äî hold it. Hard squeeze at the top ‚Äî hold it. Calves respond to time under tension, not weight. I do 15-20 reps minimum, sometimes 25-30."
  • Jay: "Standing for gastroc, seated for soleus. I'd alternate focus but hit both. Full ROM, pause at stretch, pause at contraction. No bouncing ‚Äî ever."
  • Why it matters: Small calves look worse on stage than small arms. The proportions need to match. High frequency, high reps, full ROM.

Mental Game

Mindset Tips

  • Sam: "Train the muscle, not the movement. If you can't feel it, the weight doesn't matter. Change the angle, change the grip, find the connection."
  • Jay: "There's no magic routine. The magic is showing up every day for years. I won four Olympias on consistency, not secrets."
  • Pre-workout visualization: Before you unrack, see the set. Feel the stretch. Feel the squeeze. See yourself completing the reps. Then do it.
  • When things feel heavy: Bad days happen. Reduce weight 10-15%, focus on perfect form, chase the pump. Still a productive session.
  • Mirror work: Flex between sets. Practice poses. See where the muscle is developing, where it's lacking. Stage awareness starts in the gym.
